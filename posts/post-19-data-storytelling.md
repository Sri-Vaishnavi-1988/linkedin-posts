## Post Text

The most underrated skill in data analytics:

Knowing when NOT to show a chart.

I've been in rooms where:
→ 15 charts were shown, 2 were remembered
→ A single sentence drove a  decision
→ A well-placed table beat a beautiful viz

Here's my framework before I build any visualization:

Ask: "What action do I want someone to take after seeing this?"

If the answer is:
→ "Compare values" → Bar chart
→ "See a trend" → Line chart
→ "Understand proportions" → Simple % text or pie (used sparingly)
→ "Make a specific decision" → One number, big and bold, with context

Most dashboards fail not because of the tools.
They fail because nobody asked: "So what?"

The "so what" is the whole job.

What's your #1 rule for data visualization? 👇
