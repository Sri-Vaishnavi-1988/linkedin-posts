# Post 9 — Personal Story: Almost Quit

**Category:** Story / Personal
**Status:** Pending
**Best Time to Post:** Friday 8–10 AM

## Post Text

I almost quit data analytics in my second year.

I was building reports nobody read.
Answering questions nobody asked.
Sitting in meetings where my analysis was ignored.

I thought: "Maybe I'm just not good at this."

Then a mentor told me something I'll never forget:

"You're solving data problems.
Start solving business problems.
The data is just how you prove it."

So I changed my approach.

Instead of: "Here's your weekly sales report"
I started asking: "What decision are you trying to make next week?"

Everything changed.
Stakeholders started coming to me.
My analysis started driving action.
I got promoted within 18 months.

The data never changed.
My approach did.

If you're feeling stuck right now — it's probably not your technical skills.
It's the questions you're asking.
