## Post Text

Here's the exact workflow I use to go from raw data to executive decision — every time.

Step 1: Understand the business question (Stakeholder)
Don't open SQL yet.
Ask: "What decision are you trying to make by when?"
Write it down. This is your north star.

Step 2: Pull and validate the data (SQL)
Write your query. Then QA it:
→ Row count makes sense?
→ No unexpected NULLs?
→ Numbers reconcile with a known source?

Step 3: Find the insight (SQL + Analysis)
Not "what does the data show?"
But "what does the data mean for the decision in Step 1?"

Step 4: Build the visual (Tableau)
One clear view per insight.
Design for someone who has 30 seconds, not 30 minutes.

Step 5: Communicate the recommendation (Stakeholder)
Lead with the answer.
Follow with the evidence.
End with the action.

"Revenue is declining in the West region [answer].
It's driven by a drop in repeat buyers since March [evidence].
I recommend reviewing the retention campaign for that cohort [action]."

The whole loop. Every time.

Save this framework. 🔖
