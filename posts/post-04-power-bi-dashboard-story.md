## Post Text

My stakeholder said: "I don't trust your dashboard."

Ouch.

Here's what I learned that day:

The dashboard was technically perfect.
Right numbers. Right filters. Right visuals.

But it had 14 charts on one page.
No clear title. No narrative.
Users didn't know where to look first.

I rebuilt it with one rule:
→ Every dashboard page should answer ONE question.

Page 1: "Are we hitting revenue targets this month?"
Page 2: "Where are we losing customers?"
Page 3: "Which products drive 80% of profit?"

After the rebuild?
Same data. Totally different reaction.
"This is exactly what I needed in my board meeting."

A good dashboard isn't about showing data.
It's about guiding decisions.

What's your #1 rule for dashboard design? 👇
