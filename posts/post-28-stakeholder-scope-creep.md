## Post Text

"Can we just add one more thing?"

Every data analyst has heard this.
Halfway through your dashboard build.
After you've already written 300 lines of SQL.

Here's how I handle it now (after getting burned many times):

Step 1 — Don't say yes immediately
"Let me understand what you're trying to decide with that."

Step 2 — Scope it honestly
"Adding that would take 2 extra days and push the delivery to Friday."

Step 3 — Offer a choice
"I can deliver the original scope Thursday, or the expanded scope Friday. Which matters more?"

Step 4 — Document the decision
Send a one-line email: "Confirming we're going with [option]. Delivery by [date]."

What this does:
→ Keeps you in control of scope
→ Gives stakeholders ownership of the tradeoff
→ Protects you when timelines shift
→ Builds a reputation for reliability over compliance

Saying yes to everything doesn't make you a team player.
It makes you unpredictable.

How do you handle scope creep? 👇
