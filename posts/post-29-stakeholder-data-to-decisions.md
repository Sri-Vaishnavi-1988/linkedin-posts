## Post Text

The hardest skill in data analytics has nothing to do with data.

It is translating numbers into decisions that people act on.

Here's what I've learned after hundreds of stakeholder presentations:

Nobody acts on percentages alone.
"Conversion dropped 12%"
→ So what? Is that $5K or $5M?

Nobody remembers 6 insights.
Pick ONE. Make it land.

Nobody trusts data they can't explain.
If they can't tell their manager what drove the number, they won't use it.

What works:

❌ "Activation rate is down 15% MoM"
✅ "We lost ~340 activations this month vs last. At $80 average revenue, that's $27K we didn't capture. The drop starts on the 14th — same day the onboarding email sequence changed."

Now it is not a metric.
It is a story with a villain and a possible fix.

That's what gets your analysis into the boardroom.

What's your go-to technique for making data land? 👇
