## Post Text

I Googled "am I a bad data analyst?" at 11pm last year.

The query had 847,000 results.
I felt slightly better.

Here's what imposter syndrome looks like in data:

→ Spending 3 hours on a query that takes a senior 20 minutes
→ Dreading stakeholder questions you might not know the answer to
→ Convinced everyone else understands the data better than you
→ Over-qualifying every insight with "but this might not be right"

I've felt all of these. Still do sometimes.

What actually helped:

1. I started documenting what I DID know, not just what I didn't
2. I realized "I don't know, let me find out" is a complete answer
3. I stopped comparing my chapter 3 to someone else's chapter 20

Imposter syndrome doesn't go away when you get better.
You just get better at recognizing it for what it is.

A sign you care about doing good work.

If you're feeling this way right now: you're not alone.
